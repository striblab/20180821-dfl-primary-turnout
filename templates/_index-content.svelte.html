Election 2018

DFL primary voters turned out in force — even in areas Trump won

By Chase Davis • Star Tribune

In a year with unusually robust voter turnout, the number of DFL voters in this month's statewide primary elections outpaced Republicans nearly 2:1 — including in many areas that voted for Donald Trump in 2016.

Whether that foretells a Democratic wave or is simply an isolated result is one of the key questions that will loom over the general election season as it nears its unofficial Labor Day kickoff.

Election watchers often caution against reading too much into primary election turnout, which depends heavily on party dynamics and the characteristics of individual contests. But with hotly contested governor's races at the top of each party's ticket, this year's primary offers the rare chance to compare party turnout on relatively even terms.
