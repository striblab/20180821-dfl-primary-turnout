Election 2018

DFL primary voters turned out in force — even in areas Trump won

By Chase Davis • Star Tribune

The number of DFL voters in this month's statewide primary elections outpaced Republicans nearly 2:1 — including in many areas that voted for Donald Trump in 2016.

As election season nears its unofficial Labor Day kickoff, a looming question for both parties is whether that disparity signals a wave of Democratic enthusiasm that could carry over into November, even as the state in recent years has otherwise shifted right.

Election watchers often caution against drawing broad conclusions from primary turnout, which depends heavily on party dynamics and the characteristics of individual contests. But with hotly contested governor's races at the top of each party's ticket, this year's primary offers the rare chance to compare party turnout on relatively even terms.
